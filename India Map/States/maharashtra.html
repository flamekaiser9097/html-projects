About Maharashtra

The state of Maharashtra state is India's largest industrial and commercial centre, India's gateway. The state is even famous for its cultural and religious norms. Moreover, the commercial and economic powerhouse is ranked as the 12th wealthiest global city in the New World Wealth.

Geography

The state has three crucial biogeographic zones, namely Western Ghats, Deccan Plateau, and the West coast. The Ghats nurture endemic species, Deccan Plateau provides for vast mountain ranges and grasslands while the coast is home to littoral and swamp forests. Flora of Maharashtra is heterogeneous in composition.

Tourist Attractions

  • Mumbai:The glamour of Bollywood, pulsating nightlife and romantic beaches, the mega-city of Mumbai is a mecca for dreamers looking to make it big.
  • Matheran: Blessed with a verdant topography, Matheran is India’s smallest hill station.
  • Lonavala & Khandala: Lonavala and Khandala are home to dramatic landscapes, historical forts, ancient caves.
  • Pune: Pune’s historic landmarks, hill forts, picnic spots and fabulous party places make it a favourite with the tourists for quick weekend sojourns.
  • Alibaug: Its Sun-Soaked Beaches, Historical Forts And Picturesque Picnic Spots Make It A Perfect Weekend Getaway.

Culture

The state of Maharashtra spans multiple cultures which includes cultures related to Hindus, Muslims, Buddhists, Sikhs, Christians, etc. Lord Ganesha, Maruti, Mahadeo in form of Shivlinga, Khandoba, Kalubai devi, and Lord Vitthal are some of the deities worshipped by Hindus of Maharashtra.
