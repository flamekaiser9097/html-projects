About Nagaland

Nagaland, located in northeastern India, is known as the "Land of Festivals" due to its rich cultural heritage and diverse tribal traditions. It is a state of vibrant landscapes, ranging from lush valleys to hilly terrains.

Geography

Nagaland is bordered by Assam to the west, Arunachal Pradesh and part of Assam to the north, Myanmar to the east, and Manipur to the south. Kohima, the capital, is known for its scenic beauty and pleasant climate.

Culture

Nagaland is home to 16 major tribes, each with its own distinct customs, dialects, and attire. Festivals are an integral part of life here, and the Hornbill Festival, held annually, is the most famous. Naga cuisine, with dishes like smoked meat and bamboo shoot preparations, is unique and flavorful.
