About Odisha

Odisha, formerly known as Orissa, is a state located on the eastern coast of India. Known for its ancient temples, vibrant culture, and pristine beaches, Odisha is a fascinating blend of history and modernity.

Geography

Odisha is bordered by West Bengal to the northeast, Jharkhand to the north, Chhattisgarh to the west, and Andhra Pradesh to the south. The Bay of Bengal lies to its east. Bhubaneswar, the capital, is known as the "City of Temples."

Tourist Attractions

  • Konark Sun Temple: A UNESCO World Heritage Site and architectural marvel
  • Puri: Famous for the Jagannath Temple and its serene beaches
  • Chilika Lake: Asia's largest brackish water lagoon, renowned for bird watching
  • Simlipal National Park: A biodiversity hotspot and tiger reserve
  • Udayagiri and Khandagiri Caves: Historic Jain rock-cut caves

Culture

Odisha has a rich cultural heritage with classical dance forms like Odissi. The state is famous for its festivals such as Rath Yatra, Durga Puja, and the Puri Beach Festival. Its traditional crafts, including Pattachitra painting and silver filigree work, are highly acclaimed.
