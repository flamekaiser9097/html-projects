About Tripura

Tripura, one of the smallest states in India, is known for its cultural richness and natural beauty. Nestled in the northeastern region, Tripura offers a blend of heritage, tradition, and scenic landscapes.

Geography

Tripura is bordered by Bangladesh to the north, west, and south, and the Indian states of Assam and Mizoram to the east. Agartala, the capital city, serves as the cultural and administrative hub of the state.

Tourist Attractions

  • Ujjayanta Palace: A stunning royal palace now converted into a museum
  • Neermahal: A unique lake palace located in the middle of Rudrasagar Lake
  • Unakoti: An archaeological site with rock carvings and sculptures
  • Sepahijala Wildlife Sanctuary: Home to various species of flora and fauna
  • Jampui Hills: Known for its picturesque views and orange orchards

Culture

Tripura is home to diverse communities, including indigenous tribes and Bengalis. The state is famous for its vibrant festivals like Kharchi Puja and Garia Puja. Traditional Tripuri dances and music add charm to its cultural canvas. Local handicrafts, particularly handwoven textiles and bamboo products, are widely celebrated.
