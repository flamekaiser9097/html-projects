About Karnataka

Karnataka, located in the southwestern region of India, is a state known for its rich heritage, diverse culture, and breathtaking landscapes. It is a perfect blend of modernity and tradition, offering a unique experience for visitors.

Geography

Karnataka is bordered by the Arabian Sea to the west and is home to the Western Ghats, a UNESCO World Heritage site. The state features lush green forests, waterfalls, and rivers like the Kaveri, Tungabhadra, and Krishna, which are vital to its geography.

Tourist Attractions

  • Mysore Palace: A stunning example of Indo-Saracenic architecture and the pride of Mysuru.
  • Hampi: A UNESCO World Heritage site known for its ancient temples and ruins of the Vijayanagara Empire.
  • Coorg: A serene hill station famous for its coffee plantations and scenic beauty.
  • Jog Falls: One of the highest waterfalls in India, located in the Sharavathi Valley.
  • Bannerghatta National Park: A wildlife sanctuary offering safaris and a butterfly park near Bengaluru.

Culture

Karnataka's culture is a harmonious blend of classical music, dance forms like Bharatanatyam, and festivals such as Dasara in Mysuru. The state's cuisine is diverse, featuring delicacies like Bisi Bele Bath, Mysore Pak, and Mangalorean seafood.
