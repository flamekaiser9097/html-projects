About Jharkhand

Jharkhand, often referred to as the "Land of Forests," is a state located in eastern India. Known for its rich natural resources, vibrant culture, and diverse wildlife, Jharkhand is a unique blend of tradition and modernity.

Geography

Jharkhand is bordered by Bihar to the north, Uttar Pradesh and Chhattisgarh to the west, Odisha to the south, and West Bengal to the east. Its capital, Ranchi, is known for its pleasant climate and scenic waterfalls.

Tourist Attractions

  • Betla National Park: A popular wildlife sanctuary and tiger reserve
  • Hundru Falls: A stunning waterfall near Ranchi
  • Parasnath Hill: A revered Jain pilgrimage site
  • Netarhat: Known as the "Queen of Chotanagpur," offering breathtaking views
  • Patratu Valley: A scenic destination with winding roads and lush greenery

Culture

Jharkhand is home to a diverse population, including several indigenous tribes. The state is known for its traditional dances like Chhau and Santhali. Festivals such as Sarhul, Karma, and Sohrai are celebrated with great enthusiasm. Jharkhand's handicrafts, including bamboo crafts and tribal jewelry, reflect the rich cultural heritage of the region.
