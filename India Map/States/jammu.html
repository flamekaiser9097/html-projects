About Jammu and Kashmir

Jammu and Kashmir is a region in the northern part of India, known for its stunning natural beauty, picturesque valleys, and cultural diversity. Often referred to as the "Paradise on Earth," it is famous for its pristine landscapes, snow-capped mountains, and serene lakes.

Geography

The region is divided into two main divisions:

  • Jammu: Known for its temples and vibrant culture.
  • Kashmir: Renowned for its scenic beauty, Dal Lake, and houseboats.

Tourist Attractions

  • Dal Lake and its famous houseboats
  • Gulmarg: A popular skiing destination
  • Vaishno Devi Temple: A sacred Hindu pilgrimage site
  • Pahalgam: A serene valley with lush greenery

Culture

Jammu and Kashmir is a melting pot of diverse cultures and traditions. The region is known for its traditional Kashmiri cuisine, Pashmina shawls, and vibrant festivals such as Navratri, Eid, and Lohri.
