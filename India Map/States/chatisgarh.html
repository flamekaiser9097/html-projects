About Chhattisgarh

Chhattisgarh, located in central India, is known for its rich cultural heritage, dense forests, and abundant natural resources. It is often referred to as the "Rice Bowl of India" due to its extensive paddy fields.

Geography

Chhattisgarh is bordered by Madhya Pradesh to the northwest, Maharashtra to the west, Telangana to the southwest, Odisha to the southeast, Jharkhand to the northeast, and Uttar Pradesh to the north. The state capital is Raipur, a major industrial and cultural hub.

Tourist Attractions

  • Bastar Region: Known for its tribal culture and the famous Dussehra festival
  • Chitrakote Falls: Often called the "Niagara Falls of India"
  • Kanger Valley National Park: A haven for wildlife enthusiasts
  • Sirpur: An archaeological site with ancient temples and monasteries
  • Bhoramdeo Temple: Often referred to as the "Khajuraho of Chhattisgarh"

Culture

Chhattisgarh is home to a vibrant tribal culture with traditional music, dance, and festivals. The state is known for crafts such as bell metal art, wood carving, and handwoven Kosa silk. Major festivals include Bastar Dussehra, Madai, and Teej.
