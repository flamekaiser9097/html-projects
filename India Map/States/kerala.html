Welcome to Kerala

About Kerala

Kerala, known as "God's Own Country," is a state in the southwestern part of India. It is famous for its lush greenery, tranquil backwaters, and rich cultural heritage. Kerala offers a unique experience with its serene landscapes and vibrant traditions.

Geography

Kerala is located along the Malabar Coast and is bordered by the Arabian Sea to the west. The Western Ghats, a UNESCO World Heritage site, run along its eastern border. The state is known for its backwaters, rivers, and beautiful beaches.

Tourist Attractions

  • Alleppey Backwaters: Known for its houseboat cruises and serene waterways.
  • Munnar: A picturesque hill station with sprawling tea plantations.
  • Kochi: A historic port city famous for its Chinese fishing nets and colonial architecture.
  • Wayanad: A lush green district with forests, wildlife, and waterfalls.
  • Kovalam Beach: A popular destination for sunbathing and water activities.

Culture

Kerala's culture is a blend of classical dance forms like Kathakali and Mohiniyattam, traditional music, and vibrant festivals like Onam and Vishu. The cuisine is renowned for its use of coconut, spices, and seafood, with dishes like appam, puttu, and fish curry being local favorites.
