About Bihar

Bihar, located in eastern India, is a state with a rich historical and cultural heritage. It is known as the birthplace of two major religions, Buddhism and Jainism, and boasts of ancient educational institutions like Nalanda University.

Geography

Bihar is bordered by Nepal to the north, West Bengal to the east, Uttar Pradesh to the west, and Jharkhand to the south. The fertile plains of Bihar are watered by the Ganges River, which divides the state into two regions.

Tourist Attractions

  • Bodh Gaya: A UNESCO World Heritage Site and a major pilgrimage site for Buddhists
  • Patna: The capital city, known for its historical landmarks
  • Nalanda: Famous for the ancient Nalanda University
  • Rajgir: An important site for Buddhism and Jainism
  • Vaishali: A significant site in Buddhist and Jain history

Culture

Bihar's culture is a blend of ancient traditions and modern influences. Festivals like Chhath Puja, Holi, and Diwali are celebrated with great fervor. The state is also known for its Maithili and Bhojpuri music, Madhubani paintings, and delicious cuisines such as litti chokha and sattu paratha.
