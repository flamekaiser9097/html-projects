About Manipur

Manipur, often referred to as the "Jewel of India," is a northeastern state known for its scenic landscapes, rich culture, and vibrant traditions. The state offers a perfect blend of nature's beauty and historical significance.

Geography

Manipur is bordered by Nagaland to the north, Mizoram to the south, Assam to the west, and Myanmar to the east. Its capital, Imphal, is nestled in a valley surrounded by hills and is known for its historical and cultural landmarks.

Tourist Attractions

  • Loktak Lake: The largest freshwater lake in northeastern India, known for its unique floating phumdis
  • Imphal War Cemetery: A historic site commemorating soldiers who fought in World War II
  • Kangla Fort: An ancient palace of cultural and historical importance
  • Keibul Lamjao National Park: The world's only floating national park
  • Andro Village: Known for its rich pottery traditions and tribal culture

Culture

Manipur's culture is characterized by its traditional dance forms like Ras Leela, colorful festivals such as Yaoshang and Ningol Chakouba, and its cuisine featuring dishes like Eromba and Singju. The state is also known for its handloom and handicrafts industries.
