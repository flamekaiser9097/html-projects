About Punjab

Geography

Punjab is located in the northwestern part of India. It is characterized by fertile plains and is fed by five rivers: Sutlej, Beas, Ravi, Chenab, and Jhelum. These rivers make it one of the most agriculturally rich regions of the country.

Tourist Attractions

  • Golden Temple: A spiritual and architectural marvel in Amritsar
  • Jallianwala Bagh: A historic site of great significance
  • Wagah Border: Known for the ceremonial border closing "Beating Retreat"
  • Anandpur Sahib: A holy city for Sikhs
  • Patiala: Famous for its royal heritage and traditional turban

Culture

Punjab's culture is a vibrant mix of traditions, music, dance, and cuisine. The state is famous for its Bhangra and Giddha dance forms, as well as festivals like Baisakhi and Lohri. Punjabi cuisine, including butter chicken, makki di roti, and sarson da saag, is loved across the world.
