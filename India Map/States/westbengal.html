About West Bengal

West Bengal is a state in eastern India, between the Himalayas and the Bay of Bengal. Its capital, Kolkata (formerly Calcutta), retains architectural and cultural remnants of its past as an East India Company trading post and capital of the British Raj. The city's colonial landmarks include the government buildings around B.B.D. Bagh Square, and the iconic Victoria Memorial, dedicated to Britain's queen

Geography

West Bengal is a state in eastern India, bordered by the Bay of Bengal to the south and the Himalayas to the north. It is the fourth most populated state in India.

Tourist Attractions

  • Sunderbans: UNESCO World Heritage Site housing exotic mangroves and the famous Royal Bengal Tigers.
  • Darjeeling:At the foothills of the Himalayas, Darjeeling abounds in timeless serenity.
  • Digha: It is known for its laidback beaches, picturesque vistas and diverse marine life.
  • Howrah: Howrah is a popular tourist destination that is situated on the banks of the gorgeous Hoogli River.
  • Tarapith: It is home to the renowned Tarapith Temple, where blood sacrifice of goats is a daily norm."

Culture

The culture of West Bengal is rich and diverse, with roots in Bengali literature, music, drama, fine arts, and cinema. The culture of West Bengal is influenced by the state's many tribes and ethnic groups.
