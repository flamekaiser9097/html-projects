Welcome to Meghalaya

About Meghalaya

Meghalaya, meaning "Abode of Clouds," is one of the most beautiful states in northeastern India. Known for its pristine landscapes, rolling hills, and cascading waterfalls, Meghalaya is a paradise for nature lovers.

Geography

Meghalaya is bordered by Assam to the north and east and Bangladesh to the south and west. Its capital, Shillong, is often referred to as the "Scotland of the East" due to its picturesque beauty and pleasant climate.

Tourist Attractions

  • Cherrapunji: Known for its living root bridges and record-breaking rainfall
  • Shillong: The cultural and musical hub of Meghalaya
  • Umiam Lake: A serene artificial lake surrounded by lush green hills
  • Mawsynram: The wettest place on Earth
  • Dawki: Famous for the crystal-clear waters of the Umngot River

Culture

Meghalaya is home to three major tribes: the Khasi, Garo, and Jaintia. The state celebrates vibrant festivals such as Wangala, Shad Suk Mynsiem, and Nongkrem Dance Festival. The traditional music, dance, and attire reflect the rich cultural heritage of the region. The Khasi and Jaintia tribes follow a unique matrilineal system of inheritance.
